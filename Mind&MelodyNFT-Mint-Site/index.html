Mind & Melody by NCETS

This Mind & Melody collection was developed by talented musicians who want to find a way to make positive change in their communities. Each Music NFT is designed to contribute 22% of the revenue to a mental health initiative for frontline/essential workers and their families. Our music director, Junius Bervine, has been inspired to work with very talented Web3 and Web2 artist to bring awareness to this cause by producing music in 432Hz. Our NFTs are also tiered passes packed with benefits and utilities for our IRL events. The GOLD tier is our general admission pass and  the PLATINUM tier is our VIP pass. Benefits of the passes will be shared on our social sites per event.

Meet The Artist's

Yolanda Slocum

Artist Bio:

Yolanda Y’lone Slocum, an Philadelphian. Birthed, Schooled, Inspired & Loved respectfully in the City of Brotherly Love affectionately pronounced “Norf Philly '' With early exposure to gospel music ONLY…..the “House of God, Keith Dominion” way. Steel guitars, bass, drums, cowbells and tambourines imperative and a unique sound of worship was indoctrinated early. School choir, solos and a female gospel group with her sisters/cousins eagerly learning blend, harmonies and discovering her tone as well. Living and growing up in a musical town with Soul, pop, gospel & R&B heard LIVE sparked the journey to pursue music professionally. Starting with background work for Jazmine Sullivan just 12yrs at the time. Following John Legend, Patti Labelle, Junius Bervine. TV appearances with Alicia Keys, Latoya London & Leelah James.

Zidewayz

Artist Bio:

zidewayz is the alias and name of a multi-faceted brand by queer artist Zoey Celeste Elise. Zoey uses her art as a way to express and let go of inner negativity and push through her mental health struggles; with hopes of making the lives of people around her better by spreading positivity, love and encouragement to those she meets and inspires through her art. In her own words, "I've had a difficult journey, more so than I can ever fully put into words; and its hard for me to see much hope for myself beyond the art and music that I create and the friends that I meet as zidewayz. This is everything that I have, and I want it to be something that everyone can have or be a part of. I want people to know that if they feel like they have no one in the world, or that they don't fit in anywhere; they at least have me."

Erika Hicka

Artist Bio:

New Jersey native Erika Hicks is a gifted and passionate singer-songwriter, actress, and educator. She has worked with various music recording artists including Pharrell Williams (Hidden Figures Press Tour), Bilal (Montreux Jazz Festival), The Roots (Okay Player Tour), Musiq Soulchild (Grey Goose Tour), and German music artist Joy Denelane (For the Love Tour). Erika’s distinctive songwriting is featured on jazz vocalist Laurin Talese’s album Gorgeous Chaos, her collaboration album with emerging MC Robyn Hood titled For Higher and (most recently) Philly Producer Junius Bervine's album Logical Concepts.

Brooke Taylor

Artist Bio:

Japanese American R&B sensation, Brooke Taylor, is making her mark on the industry one strategic step at a time. The Los Angeles born singer released her first album “Live On Virtuous Energy (L.O.V.E) that quickly gained recognition from some of the most popular DJs and industry professionals. She was first heard vocally competing on the Steve Harvey Morning show. She later was crowned the “Neighborhood Idol” by a celebrity panel on Big Boy’s Morning Show. Taylor released her 2nd album “iHuman” under the band name of Venus/Mars premiering their first single “Forgiveness” via the band’s Vevo page.

Ayotemi

Artist Bio:

An African prince of the Indigo generations. Ayotemi is a Nigerian-American, independent artist and songwriter hailing from Laurel, Maryland. Not missing a beat he carries a range that speaks to his Nigerian-American roots as well as his DMV upbringing. The young artist carries influence from legends like Bob Marley, Fela Kuti, D'angelo and newer acts like Smino, Saba, Baby Keem and Chance the Rapper others. He continues to reinvent his work style and match his cadence with a sound that’s a fusion of soul, hip hop, and afro-beats.

Immanu El

Artist Bio:

Raised in a family of singers and musicians as well as the baptist church, music always played a major role in his life. Instruments such as the drums and keys came naturally as he was self taught. Before officially becoming an artist, Immanu El began his career in beat making and is still predominantly a music producer. His music is a reflection of his spiritual journey, with each level of awareness being documented through song. Travels to Peru, Egypt and Ethiopia over a period of nine years broadened his horizon and a more self aware and cultured artist blossomed. Within the wordplay and punchlines is always a message.
